MOROCCAN WEDDINGS

Sought-after for its year-round sunshine and vibrant nuptial traditions, Morocco is the perfect place to celebrate with your loved ones as you take your first steps as a married couple. Whether you picture your ceremony on a tranquil beach, within a blooming Andalusian garden, or surrounded by the Atlas mountains, Experience Morocco can create an unforgettable celebration for you and your family. From palatial resorts to sumptuous Riads, your destination wedding can be as ornate or simple as you desire, and we can include elements of Moroccan traditions like a henna ceremony or the traditional lifting of the wedded couple during the party. With an international team of travel professionals and a breadth of experience in curated luxury travel, Experience Morocco is committed to making your wedding a huge success!


Host Cities

More

Morocco is full of palatial resorts, charming riads, and gorgeous natural landscapes perfect for hosting a wedding. The following slides present a few cities where we can organize your big day.

Casablanca

Casablanca embodies modern Morocco. It’s impossible to feel indifferent to this vibrant city. Be sure to take in its “towers”: the minaret of the Hassan II mosque, the El Hank lighthouse, and the Twin Center. Casablanca has a very rich architectural heritage, much of which remains to be explored; not to mention a stroll along the Boulevard de la Corniche for the beach-side atmosphere.

Marrakech

Nicknamed the Ochre City, Marrakech is an established destination for romantic getaways. The city’s charming alleyways, splendid colours and scents will awaken your senses to a delightful world where modern culture sits just on the other side of 11th century walls.

The Atlas Mountains

A Wedding in Morocco’s Atlas Mountains is an unforgettable experience. The beauty and diversity of the landscape is breathtaking. Its mix of berber villages, deep lush valleys and snow-capped mountains accounts for a splendid Alpine experience.

Tangier

Tangier’s charms have inspired artists, writers and travelers from Henri Matisse to Tennessee Williams. With a beautiful bay overlooking the Strait of Gibraltar, Tangier offers the perfect backdrop to your Mediterranean wedding. The city features several world-class resorts, a beautiful art scene as well beaches you won’t soon forget!

Essaouira

Essaouira’s brilliant blue and white architecture, windswept beaches and easy-going charm provide the perfect scenery for an authentic Moroccan wedding. Behind the 18th century walls are artisan workshops, souks, and winding alleyways that embody the charms of an intimate venue straight out of the Arabian Nights’ fairy tale.

Moroccan Wedding Traditions

Hammam

Before the wedding, the bride and her intimate friends meet for the traditional Hammam bath. Authentic black soap and purifying scrubs give rise to incredible scents of eucalyptus, honey and orange blossom. The cheering chants and relaxed atmosphere raise everyone’s spirits for the big day!

Henna Ceremony

After the Hammam bath, the henna ceremony or "Henna" is held in the presence of family and friends. The bride’s hands are adorned with a traditional tattoo made of Henna and herbal essences. The symbolic virtues of henna in a Moroccan wedding is to bring health, prosperity and success to the newlyweds.

Negafates

During the ceremony, the bride is assisted by "Neggafates" who are logistic experts in traditional weddings. The Neggafates’ expertise in traditional ceremonies, style and fashion enables them to help with traditional Moroccan dresses, jewelry, hair styling and even the lifting of the bride in the “Amariya”.

Amariya

The “Amariya” is the traditional cushion table where the bride sits comfortably before she gets lifted by the Negaffates. Adorned with a traditional dress of ceremony, the bride is touring as to be seen and admired by the entire audience in a festive atmosphere full of sound and color.

Sbouhi - Post Wedding Brunch

Breakfast in bed is nice, but a Moroccan brunch with the wedding bunch can be a blissful experience! Traditionally, the “Sbouhi” or post-wedding Brunch brings together family and friends. Gifts and blessings are shared with the newlyweds as they make their way to their idyllic honeymoon.

Aissawa

The “Aissawa are known for their polyrhythmic spiritual music, which generally comprises songs accompanied by percussion and dance. Aissawa’s presence in weddings is a symbol of good luck and prosperity, as the group brings together traditional music traditions and dances to elevate the participants to a state of joyful excitement.

The Meal

Moroccan weddings are known for the variety of their exotic dishes and authentic ingredients. Traditionally, wedding meals include a pastilla (chicken puff pastry with almonds, sugar and cinnamon), a Mashwi (baby lamb), a Tajine (meat with prunes and almonds), or Couscous. Moroccan pastries and cookies are also served with mint tea throughout the ceremony.

Beyond The Wedding

Jam with a Gnawa Band

Gnawa music is a rich repertoire of African mystic songs and rhythms. Its well preserved heritage combines ritual poetry with traditional music and dancing. Jam with a local tribal band under the starry sky of the Sahara Desert or at Jemaa El Fna, Marrakech's Central square for a vibrant experience you’ll never forget.

Moroccan Gourmet Workshop

Pick your fresh fish from the port of Essaouira, shop for spices in the busy souks of Marrakech, or bake bread in the way of the nomads in the hot sand of the Sahara. Our cooking workshop will take you and your guests on a sensory adventure to discover, taste, and learn Morocco’s delicious cuisine.

Camel Rides

Enjoy a quintessential Moroccan experience with your friends and family on the back of camel, through the rugged landscapes around Marrakech or the Saharan dunes. For those who haven’t ridden a camel before, the experience of getting on and off the camel is a sensation unlike any other!

Sahara Adventures

Embark on a Saharan adventure to sandboard down the dunes, watch the sunset on camelback, and enjoy a magical night under the stars from your private bedouin camp

Nightlife

With a wide array of international restaurants, clubs, and cabarets featuring world-class entertainment, Morocco’s nightlife offers something for everyone and we will go above and beyond to make sure you and your guests have the best time once the sun goes down!

Hike in the Atlas Mountains

Morocco’s wealth of landscapes provides the perfect backdrop for a memorable hike through the Atlas mountains, stopping at local Berber villages for a taste of a lifestyle unchanged for centuries.
